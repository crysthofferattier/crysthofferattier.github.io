Ohm's Law

Ohm's law states that the electric current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points. Introducing the constant of proportionality, the resistance,[1] one arrives at the three mathematical equations used to describe this relationship



Formula

V = I × R

Explanation

  • Voltage (V): The push that moves electric charges.
  • Current (I): The flow of those charges.
  • Resistance (R): How much the flow is opposed.

Examples

Finding Current

Scenario: You have a circuit with a voltage of 12 volts and a resistance of 4 ohms.

Calculation: Using Ohm's Law:

I = V / R = 12 volts / 4 ohms = 3 amps

Interpretation: The current flowing through the circuit is 3 amps.

Finding Voltage

Scenario: You have a device that draws 2 amps of current with a resistance of 6 ohms.

Calculation: V = I × R = 2 amps × 6 ohms = 12 volts

Interpretation: The voltage needed to maintain that current is 12 volts.

Finding Resistance

Scenario: A circuit has a voltage of 24 volts and a current of 8 amps.

Calculation: R = V / I = 24 volts / 8 amps = 3 ohms

Interpretation: The resistance in the circuit is 3 ohms.
