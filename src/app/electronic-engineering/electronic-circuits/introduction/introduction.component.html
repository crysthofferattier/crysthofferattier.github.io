Introduction to Electronics

Electronics is the branch of science and technology that deals with the flow of electric current and the manipulation of electrical energy. It forms the foundation of modern technology, enabling everything from basic household appliances to complex computer systems and communication networks.

At its core, electronics involves the study of components such as resistors, capacitors, diodes, and transistors, which are used to control and direct electrical current. These components can be combined into circuits that perform various functions, from simple tasks like lighting a bulb to sophisticated operations in smartphones and medical devices.
